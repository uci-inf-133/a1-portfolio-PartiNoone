Traditional Art

Pencil and paper. Or pen. Or marker. Or paint. Or-

Radicals: Black and white shapes that invert the text color? On paper? Cool to look at, but a pain to do if you had no markers and no white gel pen. You can tell I got sick of it after the first box when I switched to solid white text boxes instead.

Logarithms: Another page, more of the same.

Factoring: This one was much nicer on my hand muscles. Using colored pencils to color in blocks of paper is definitely preferable to using pen to do the same thing.

Blueprint: This one started as an assignment for Digital Media class, but it was really complicated and it seemed like too much work to recreate the whole thing in Adobe Illustrator, so this became its own thing. The drawing I used for the assignment was vaguely similar in aesthetic, so that's why this drawing kind of looks like the second-last image on the Digital Art page.
